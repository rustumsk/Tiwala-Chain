# TiwalaChain Interface System

## Direction And Feel

- Treat the product as an escrow operations workspace, not a generic crypto dashboard.
- The primary user question is always: what money is locked, what action is blocking release, and which jobs need attention now?
- The interface should feel controlled, credible, and operational rather than flashy or decorative.

## Product World

- Domain concepts: escrow, release approvals, counterparties, funded work, milestone progression, on-chain verification, settlement risk, trust signals.
- Color world: vault black, receipt-paper neutrals, notarized violet, stablecoin green, review amber, dispute red.
- Signature pattern: an escrow rail that makes the lifecycle legible across overview surfaces and job flows.

## Depth Strategy

- Use borders-first depth with subtle same-hue surface shifts.
- Avoid strong shadows, loud gradients, or separate color worlds for shell vs content.
- Violet is for trust and verification, green is for released value, amber is for pending review, red is for disputes or failures.

## Spacing And Type

- Use a 4px spacing base and favor tighter operational density over oversized marketing spacing.
- Keep typography precise and calm with strong weight hierarchy.
- Use tabular numbers for financial values and counts whenever possible.

## Reusable Patterns

- Lead dashboards with one dominant escrow overview instead of a row of identical metric cards.
- Prefer lifecycle summaries, action queues, and exception counts over generic KPIs.
- Keep quick actions attached to workflow progression, not as isolated utility tiles.
- Maintain the same dark surface family across the app shell and page content.
